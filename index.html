Frequently asked questions

A Credit Builder Account is a small installment loan held by a bank. Your monthly payments are reported to all three credit bureaus — Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion — helping you establish positive payment history. At the end of the term, you receive your savings minus fees.

Self reports your monthly payments to all three major credit bureaus. Making consistent, on-time payments helps establish a positive credit history, which is a key factor in your credit score. Over time, this can help you improve your credit profile.

If you're building credit from scratch, start with a Credit Builder Account. If you already have a Self account and want spending power, unlock the Self Visa® Credit Card. For rent reporting, our Rent & Bills feature reports your payments to all 3 bureaus automatically.

Self members see an average credit score increase of 47 points. Results vary based on individual credit profiles and factors. Self helps build positive payment history, which makes up 35% of your FICO® score calculation.
